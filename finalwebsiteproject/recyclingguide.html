WHAT SHOULD I RECYCLE?

ACCEPTED ON BIN

Plastic Bottles and Containers

Recycle plastics by shape: bottles, jars, jugs and tubs.

Cardboard and Paperboard

Flatten and recycle all cardboard and paperboard.

Cardboard and Paperboard

Flatten and recycle all cardboard and paperboard.

Food and Beverages Containers

Make sure containers are completely empty to avoid contamination.

Food and Beverages Cans

Recycle empty tin, aluminum and steel cans.

Paper

Recycle empty tin, aluminum and steel cans.

NOT ACCEPTED ON BIN

Plastic Wrap and Film

Plastic wrap, bubble wrap, sandwich bags and freezer bags should not go in the recycle bin.

Polystyrene Foam and Plastic

Polystyrene foam, plastic "to-go" containers and cups are made of non-recyclable materials.

Plastic Bags

They get tangled in equipment and threaten worker safety.Reuse plastic bags.

Cups with Wax or Plastic Coatings

Cups with plastic or waxed coatings are not recyclable.Invest in and carry a reusable cup instead.

Medical Waste

Medical waste can pose a safety hazard and never goes in curbside recycling or waste bins.

Garage Waste

Car parts, scrap metal, tires, filters, propane cylinders and the like are safety hazards if put in curbside recycling or trash bins.
